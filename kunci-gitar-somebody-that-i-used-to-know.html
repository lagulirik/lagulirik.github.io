5. Now I know that'should' is used to tell someone to do somethingFor exampleNow I know that shouldn't is used to tell someone not to do somethingFor example​


Jawaban:

To say something is necessary

Must

We use “must” to talk about obligations. Often, when we use “must”, the authority for the obligation comes from the person who is speaking.

Examples:

“You must do your homework every night.” (Because I say you must!)

“I must stop smoking!” (Because I think it’s a good idea to stop.)

Remember that “must” is a modal auxiliary verb. This means that it doesn’t change its ending (I must, he must, etc) and that it’s followed by the infinitive without ‘to’. (“You must phone me” not “You must to phone me”.)

For more information on modal auxiliary verbs, see our page on ability.

Have to

We can also use “have to” to talk about rules and regulations. The authority for the obligation doesn’t come from the person who is speaking. Perhaps the rule is a general law or obligation.

“In England you have to pay tax.”

“We have to check everyone’s ID.”

“Have to” is a normal verb. Use “do” or “does” to make a question, and “don’t” or “doesn’t” to make a negative.

“Do you have to vote in an election?”

“He doesn’t have to wear a uniform to school.”

Have got to

“Have got to” is common in British English and is stronger than “have to”.

“I have got to fill in this form. The deadline is tomorrow.”

“She has got to study hard to pass the exam.”

To make the question and negative form, use “have”, “has”, “haven’t” and “hasn’t”:

“Have you got to leave early tomorrow?”

6. Which is the sentence used to know that someone has known something clearly. *A. Yes. I doB. I am willing to explain the C.new topic to you.D. Do you understand?Please, give me attention!*​


"D" option is the correct answer.

EXPLANATION

When we want to know that someone has clearly known what we have just said is using the expression of checking for understanding.

Dalam Bahasa Indonesia, ketika kita ingin mengetahui lawan bicara kita sudah paham apa yang telah kita katakan adalah dengan menggunakan ungkapan pengecekan pemahaman.

Here are the examples of checking understanding :

~ Do you understand what I'm saying

(Apakah kamu ngerti apa yg sy katakan?)

~ Are you with me?

(Apakah kamu ngerti)

~ Do you know what I'm talking about

(Apakah kamu ngerti apa yg sy sedang bicarakan)

~ Do you know what I mean

(Apakah kamu ngerti apa yg sy maksudkan)

~ Any questions ?

(Ada pertanyaan nggak?)

~ Have you got it?

(Apakah kamu ngerti?)

~ Do you get the point?

(Apakah kamu paham)

ELABORATION

Which is the sentence used to know that someone has known something clearly.

(Kalimat mana yang digunakan untuk mengetahui bahwa seseorang telah paham sesuatu dengan jelas)

A. Yes. I do (Ya)

~ Opsi A. tidak cocok karena ini merupakan tanggapan ketika ada pengajuan pertanyaan.

B. I am willing to explain the new topic to you. (Aku berniat untuk menjelaskan topik baru ke kamu)

~ Opsi B. tidak cocok karena ungkapan ini merupakan niatan untuk melakukan sesuatu (expression of willingness)

C. Please, give me attention (Tolong, minta perhatiannya)

~ Opsi C. tidak cocok karena ungkapan ini digunakan ketika ingin meminta perhatian (checking for attention)

D. Do you understand? (Apakah kamu paham)

~ Opsi D adalah jawaban yg benar karena ungkapan ini digunakan ketika kita ingin memeriksa pemahaman lawan bicara atas ucapan yg kita sampaikan.

Commonly Confused Words Used to vs Be used to

I used to walk to school.

I used to listen to pop music.

I used to drink milk in the morning.

I am used to cycling to school.

I am used to listening to rock music.

I am used to drinking coffee in the morning.

-----------

Explanation

Used to adalah phrasal verb yang digunakan untuk mengekspresikan suatu kebiasaan di masa lalu yang tidak lagi dilakukan. Used to diikuti bare infinitive.

Be used to digunakan untuk mengekspresikan sesuatu yang telah menjadi kebiasaan/normal, atau menjadi suatu yang biasa dilakukan padahal sebelumnya tidak. Be used to diikuti gerund.

Bandingkan

When I was in Indonesia, I used to drive on the left side of the road. (Saya tidak lagi di Indonesia, dan saya tidak lagi menyetir di sisi kiri)

I'm from Indonesia. I am used to driving on the left side of the road. (Saya dari Indonesia jadi saya terbiasa menyetir di sisi kiri).
